Pike & Squat

Two skill-focused sessions targeting hamstrings, calves, ankles, hips, and adductors. Three to five stretches each, stretch-stacked, run one to two times per week.

A · Pike B · Squat Schedule
Session A

Pike

Targets — hamstrings, gastrocnemius, sciatic nerve, posterior chain
01
Sciatic Nerve Floss
2 × 10 reps per leg
Clears nerve tension before stretching, so the hamstring work later actually goes deeper. Nerves slide rather than stretch — never push into pain.
02
Supine Hamstring Stretch — Strap
3 × 30s per side
Dorsiflex the foot hard to include the gastroc. Keep the non-working leg flat on the floor — bending it tilts the pelvis and cheats the stretch.
03
Straight-Knee Wall Calf Stretch
3 × 30s per side
Gastroc crosses the knee and limits pike depth — opens the next exercise. Keep the back knee locked and the heel pinned to the floor.
04
Seated Pike Compression — Isometric Reps
3 × 6 reps · ~4s hold
Resisted training in the new range. Hands on shins or feet, actively pull yourself down. For loaded version: heavy plate or DB across thighs, push legs up into it for 5s × 6.
05
Jefferson Curl
3 × 5 slow reps · 5–10 kg
Loaded flexion through full range. Roll down vertebra by vertebra, chin to chest first. Start unloaded for 1–2 sessions to learn the segmental movement before adding weight.
Session B

Deep Squat

Targets — ankle dorsiflexion, hip rotation, adductors
01
Knee-to-Wall Ankle Mob
2 × 10 reps per side
Drive the knee past the toes, rebuild dorsiflexion. Heel stays down — don't let it lift to chase depth. Track knee straight over middle toe.
02
Frog Stretch
3 × 45s · rock gently
Adductors and internal hip rotation. Knees wide, feet flexed, shins parallel. Rock back and forth to find depth — don't force a static end-range hold.
03
90/90 Hip Switches
3 × 6 reps per side · pause at end range
External and internal hip rotation. Sit tall — don't collapse the back hip. Lean forward over the front shin to deepen, lean back to load the trail leg.
04
Deep Goblet Squat Hold
3 × 30s
Integrates the position. Elbows pressing knees out, weight through whole foot, chest tall. Use a moderate weight — enough to anchor you down without crushing posture.
05
Tempo Goblet Squat — 3s Pause
3 × 6 reps · 3s bottom pause
Strengthens the range. Same setup as the hold — descend controlled, pause 3s at the bottom, drive up. No video needed: it's the hold above with reps stacked on either side.

Weekly Layout

Mobility is fatiguing — same mechanism as strength training. Schedule it like real work, not active recovery.

SUN — 2 days post-Friday strength. Best window. Run Session A or B fresh.

WED PM — Same day as strength, hours apart. Second-best window. Brain fatigue hasn't fully landed.

AVOID — Tue, Thu, Sat. Day-after strength = aphereent fatigue signals limit your stretch tolerance.

If running once weekly, prioritise Sunday and alternate A / B each week.
